About Me

I'm Allen Joshua — a Data Analyst and Data Science graduate student at the University of Texas at Arlington focused on building intelligent, data-driven systems that go beyond traditional analytics. My work sits at the intersection of data analytics, AI-powered decision systems, machine learning, and business intelligence — combining SQL, Python, Power BI, NLP, and modern generative AI workflows to transform complex data into actionable strategy.

What sets me apart is my ability to bridge analytical thinking with real-world problem solving in an AI-driven landscape. While modern AI tools can generate outputs, I focus on designing systems that ask better questions, validate information critically, interpret business context, and turn uncertainty into structured decision-making. I approach data not just as numbers to visualize, but as signals that need reasoning, evaluation, and human-centered interpretation.

Recently, I've been building projects centered around AI robustness, strategic decision intelligence, and applied machine learning — including systems that evaluate adversarial reliability in Vision Transformers and AI-powered platforms that synthesize real-time information into structured recommendations, comparison frameworks, and analytical insights.

I'm driven by curiosity, experimentation, and the belief that the future belongs to professionals who can combine data, AI, and critical thinking into systems that genuinely help people make better decisions.
